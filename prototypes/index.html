Design 01 / 05

Liquid Glass

Glassmorphism · Dark Mode

Deep navy animated mesh gradients with frosted-glass panels and dual cyan + amber accent system. Orbitron headings evoke precision; Cormorant Garamond brings warmth. The night atmosphere conjures stargazing from a Norman farmhouse — a premium, otherworldly feel that stands out in the rural accommodation market.

  • Animated gradient mesh creates instant visual impact on load
  • Frosted glass cards feel inherently premium and contemporary
  • Dual accent system (cyan + amber) maps perfectly to sea and harvest
  • Booking flow highlighted with glowing CTA that demands attention

Design 02 / 05

Neo-Brutalist

Editorial · Dark Mode

Bebas Neue headlines at 100px+ with raw black and electric yellow. No border-radius, no gentle softening — just brutal typographic hierarchy and thick rule-lines. Subverts the cosy B&B cliché by presenting Normandy with the confidence of an independent magazine. Unforgettable for the right guest: the discerning traveller who sees through sentiment.

  • Typographic scale alone creates hierarchy without decorative noise
  • Yellow accent on dark guarantees maximum contrast and legibility
  • Raw editorial aesthetic positions the property as cultural, not quaint
  • IBM Plex Mono data tables give rate cards authority and clarity

Design 03 / 05

Bento Spatial Light

Bento Grid · Light Mode

Clean white canvas with a varied bento grid layout — cards of asymmetric heights and widths create natural rhythm without chaos. Plus Jakarta Sans is contemporary without clinical coldness. Apple-blue accent works universally. The grid's spatial composition turns the POI explorer into a genuine discovery experience, and the rates feel transparent rather than transactional.

  • Bento grid asymmetry guides the eye naturally through all content
  • Light mode maximises approachability for a broad guest demographic
  • Apple-blue system colour feels instantly credible and trustworthy
  • Micro-shadows and subtle dividers create depth without visual noise

Design 04 / 05

Cinematic Luxury

Editorial Dark · Gold Accent

The film-poster aesthetic: deep charcoal and burnt black with gold leaf typography, full-bleed imagery framed by diagonal clip-paths. Cormorant Garamond at large scale reads like a fashion spread. Every section transition is choreographed. Targets the high-value guest researching a special occasion stay — anniversary, D-Day pilgrimage, cultural retreat — who equates beauty with trust.

  • Diagonal clip-path section cuts create cinematic forward momentum
  • Gold on deep charcoal triggers luxury associations immediately
  • Cormorant Garamond at display scale is deeply distinctive
  • Seasonal rate cards presented as a premium offering, not a price list

Design 05 / 05

Terrain · Organic

Natural · Warm Light Mode

Warm parchment canvas with topographic SVG patterns, offset card shadows, and Playfair Display serifs. Feels handmade in the best sense — like a lovingly crafted brochure found in a village auberge. Nunito body text softens the serif's formality. The most natural fit for a rural Normandy farmhouse: honest, warm, inviting. No artifice. The trails timeline for POIs feels like a walking guide.

  • Parchment palette and serif type are authentic to the location
  • Topographic SVG texture adds depth without photographic assets
  • Offset card shadows create tactile, hand-placed composition feel
  • POI trail timeline mirrors the guest journey through Normandy
